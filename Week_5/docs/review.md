### The Big Picture

You pick **one dataset**, analyze it through 5 steps, and produce **3 outputs**. That's it.

### Step 1: Pick a dataset

- **Bank Marketing** → "why do customers subscribe to term deposits?"
- **Telco Churn** → "why are customers leaving?"

Churn tends to be a bit more intuitive to reason about and has cleaner storytelling (lose a customer vs win one), but either works.

### The 5 analysis steps (this is your working process, not separate deliverables)

|Step|What you actually do|
|---|---|
|1. Define the problem|Write 2-3 sentences: what business question are you answering?|
|2. Explore the data|Rows/columns, data types, missing values, summary stats, distributions — with charts|
|3. Find key drivers|Which columns actually correlate with the outcome (e.g. tenure, contract type for churn)|
|4. Turn findings into insights|Plain-English "so what" statements a manager could understand|
|5. Recommendations|3-5 concrete actions based on what you found|

This is the same shape as the Superstore project you just did — problem → explore → drivers → insights → recommendations. You already have the muscle memory for this.

### The 3 deliverables (outputs of that process)

1. **Report** (3-5 pages) — Intro, Problem, Dataset Description, Analysis Process, Findings, Insights, Recommendations, Conclusion
2. **Presentation** (5-10 slides) — condensed version of the report, visual-first
3. **LinkedIn post** — dataset, insights, recommendations, skills used, hashtags including #AnalystLabAfrica
